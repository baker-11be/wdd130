Insight: Central corridor (Kampala, Masaka, Kyoga) exhibits very high rates (>30/100k). Rural areas like Kidepo, Katonga remain low (<7/100k).
Recommendations & Policy Implications
Recommendations: 1) Deploy predictive policing & CCTV expansion in high-risk regions (KMP North, Rwizi, North Kyoga). 2) Integrate economic empowerment programs in moderate-risk zones to reduce urban crime drivers. 3) Strengthen community policing in low-risk zones to maintain stability & underreporting reduction. 4) Establish annual CVI monitoring dashboard for resource allocation.
Discussions: Findings confirm Routine Activity Theory: rapid urbanization and unemployment correlate with crime clusters. Domestic violence & economic offences dominate central regions, whereas rural areas face distinct challenges (cattle rustling, narcotics). Underreporting remains a limitation, yet composite index provides robust risk stratification.
Conclusions: Uganda's crime landscape is spatially polarized. High-risk zones demand urgent multi-sectoral intervention. Safe zones demonstrate resilience through social cohesion. Data-driven forecasts (ARIMA) suggest 2-3% annual increase in high-urban regions by 2026 unless targeted prevention scales. The Navigator empowers evidence-based peace building aligned with SDG 16.
